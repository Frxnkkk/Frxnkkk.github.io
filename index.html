From ASR to TTS: Enhancing Synthesis with Cleaned ASR Data

Abstract

This paper investigates a novel method for handling with the scarcity of appropriate speech corpora for Text-to Speech (TTS) systems in the Thai language by repurposing Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) corpus. In this paper, we focus on whether noisy ASR by exploiting the Miipher speech restoration model approach and examine its suitability for TTS corpus generation.Our study compares single-speaker and multi-speaker Tacotron2 models trained on both original and Miipher-processed datasets. Through a series of experiments including A/B preference tests and Mean Opinion Score (MOS) evaluations, we demonstrate the efficacy of our approach. Results show a clear preference for Miipher-processed data over original noisy data, with 67.65% of participants favoring the processed audio. As expectedly, given the limited amount of data per speaker in the multi-speaker dataset, the single-speaker TTS model outperformed the multi-speaker model in preference tests, with 63.55% of participants preferring the single-speaker system. MOS evaluations revealed that Miipher-processed data achieved the highest score of 3.69 ± 0.15, followed by the original noisy data at 3.56 ± 0.17, while both TTS systems showed lower scores. These findings indicate that repurposing ASR data with appropriate processing can effectively improve speech quality, but translating this improvement to TTS systems requires further investigation. Our results suggest that the quantity of per-speaker data may be more crucial than speaker diversity for TTS development using processed ASR data.
